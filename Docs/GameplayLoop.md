# Gameplay Loop

The core gameplay loop of SCRAPSHIFT is structured around the concept of a working "Shift".

## 1. Base Hub
The central gathering point for the crew between missions.
- Manage loadouts.
- Purchase upgrades using earned money.
- Interact with other players.

## 2. Preparation
- Select tools and equipment for the upcoming shift.
- Review any known modifiers or intelligence about the next ship.

## 3. Mission Selection
- Choose the next ship to repair.
- Difficulty and potential payouts scale up as players progress.

## 4. Flight & Arrival
- The team travels to the target.
- The ship layout, failures, and tasks are procedurally generated during this phase.

## 5. Arrival & Dark Ship
- Players spawn in the drop zone.
- The ship is completely unpowered (no lights, no doors, no task UI).

## 6. Reactor Initialization
- Players must navigate in the dark to the reactor room.
- Manually start the reactor to restore power to the grid.

## 7. Task Assignment
- With power restored, the ship's diagnostic interface boots up.
- The crew receives their prioritized list of repair tasks (Critical, High, Medium, Low).
- Mission timers begin.

## 8. Repair Phase
- Players split up or work together to navigate the ship.
- Engage in system-specific minigames to repair generators, seal leaks, fix doors, etc.
- Manage critical tasks before their timers expire to prevent immediate failure.

## 9. Mission Completion
- All mandatory tasks are completed.
- The ship is stabilized.

## 10. Evacuation
- Return to the drop zone/shuttle.
- Extract from the repaired ship.

## 11. Reward & Progression
- Receive monetary payout based on completed tasks, speed, and difficulty.
- Return to the Base Hub to spend earnings on upgrades (tools, speed, inventory, cosmetics).
- Prepare for the next, harder shift.
